Frontend Accessibility. Part_1

Common Assistive Technologies

Screen Controls

  • Allows users with motor impairments to control the site without the use of a touchscreen, mouse, or keyboard.

Screen Reader

  • Software that reads web content aloud to assist visually impaired users.

Switch Magnifier

  • Used to enlarge content for users with sight impairments.

Closed Captioning

  • Make videos accessible to deaf and hard-of-hearing users, as well as users with Auditory Processing Disorder.

Vocabulary

Accessible Design:

  • A design process in which the needs of people with disabilities are specifically considered.
  • Better overall usability.
  • Reach a larger audience.
  • Search engine optimization (SEO) standards.

Curb-cut-effect:

  • Accessibility has benefits beyond allowing people with disabilities to use a product/service.

Americans with disabilities act, title III

  • Websites and apps are places of public accommodation.
  • Prevents discrimination against people with disabilities.
  • Microsoft inclusive design toolkit.
