Perceivable Header 1

Perceivable Principle

  • Information and user interface components must be presentable to users in ways they can perceive.
  • Provide alternative methods for understanding web content.
  • Ensure text remains legible at 200% zoom.
  • Responsive body copy
  • Avoid autostarting audio or video
  • Combine multiple visual cues, not just color
  • Sufficient color contrast
  • Portrait and landscape orientation
  • Captions and transcripts
  • Use semmantic HTML
Blind users:
Use listening methods and screen readers to access content.
Deaf or hearing loss users:
Provide closed captioning and transcriptions within media.

Perceivable Guidelines for Accessibility Compliance

  1. Alternative Text

    • Enhances accessibility and SEO.
    • Supports screen readers.
    • Improves engagement.
    • Helps categorize and rank images.
    • Addresses image load issues.
    • Assists users with low or impaired vision.
    • Effective text alternative solutions for non-text content
    Alternative attributes:
    Short descriptions added to images on a webpage.
    Empty alternative attributes:
    Use alt="" with decorative images to tell screen readers to ignore them.
    Rules, Guidelines, and Best Practices for Alternative Text
    • Be descriptive but concise.
    • Focus on purpose, not just appearance.
    • Avoid unnecessary phrases.
    • Use keywords naturally.
    • Handle decorative images properly.
    • Provide extra content for complex images.
    • Include alt text for buttons.
    • Use CSS for purely decorative images.
    Different Image Types Require Different Alternative Texts
    • Logos
    • Charts and graphs
    • Icons and buttons
    • People
    • Decorative images
    Common Mistakes in Alternative Text
    • Being too vague.
    • Over-explaining.
    • Keyword stuffing.
    • Neglecting important images.

  2. Captions and Transcripts

    Time-based Media:
    Provide captions and transcripts for audio and video to assist users with hearing loss or auditory processing disorders.
    Best Practices for Time-based Media
    • Limit captions to two lines at once.
    • Allow draggable captions to avoid blocking text.
    • Review automatic captions and transcripts.
    • Use external transcription services when needed.

  3. Use Semantic HTML

    Adaptable:
    Content should be presentable in multiple ways without losing meaning.

  4. Support Portrait and Landscape Orientation

    Adaptable:
    Ensure content works in both portrait and landscape orientations.
